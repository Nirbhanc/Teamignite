WHAT IS STEM RACING?

A premier global student competition where teams use industry-standard technology — including CAD modeling, CFD fluid dynamics, CNC machining, and 3D printing — to design, manufacture, and race miniature F1 cars. Beyond engineering, teams compete in a full Enterprise Challenge: building a real business case with sponsorships, branding, financial planning, and a professional portfolio presentation judged by industry experts.

WHO WE ARE

We are a dedicated engineering team from SATEC @ W.A. Porter CI in Toronto. As a proud public school team, we rely on passion, resilience, and hard work to overcome challenges and compete for National Championships.

OUR JOURNEY

From a spark to a flame — the story behind the team.

2024
THE ORIGIN

NITRO RACING

Before Ignite, there was Nitro. A group of students from SATEC @ W.A. Porter CI built their first car from scratch, learned the craft, and shocked everyone by claiming the 2024 Canadian Checkpoint Championship — placing 2nd in all of North America.

2025
THE BREAKTHROUGH

CANADIAN NATIONALS

Nitro Racing entered the 2025 Canadian Nationals and stood on top of the podium as National Champions. The team qualified for the World Finals — but without funding, the dream had to wait.

2025
THE REBRAND

BORN AS IGNITE

With most original members heading to university, a fresh generation stepped up. The team rebranded as Team Ignite — a new name, a new identity, and a burning ambition. They placed 4th at Regionals and punched through to Nationals.

2026
THE PINNACLE

NATIONAL CHAMPIONS

In their debut season as Team Ignite, they swept the competition — taking home the 2026 Canadian National Championship alongside Best Engineered Car, Best Enterprise Portfolio, and Best Team Identity.

NOW
WHAT'S NEXT

THE WORLD STAGE

The story isn't over. Team Ignite is deep in engineering, testing, and preparation as they set their sights on the World Finals. The flame is burning brighter than ever.

CANADIAN NATIONAL CHAMPIONS 2026

CANADIAN NATIONALS 2025

In the 2025 season, competing as Nitro Racing, our team entered the Canadian National Championships and claimed 1st place in Canada, finishing 2nd in all of North America. This victory laid the foundation for what would become Team Ignite — and set the stage for the 2026 sweep.

IGNITE ON CTV

Being featured on CTV was a massive milestone for Team Ignite. It gave us the platform to show the province how pushing the boundaries of CAD, CFD, and aerodynamics can translate from a high school classroom directly into the world of elite engineering.

MONTREAL GRAND PRIX

To truly understand the physics of racing, we went straight to the source. Attending the Formula 1 Canadian Grand Prix allowed us to analyze full-scale aerodynamics in action and network with the masterminds behind the sport.
